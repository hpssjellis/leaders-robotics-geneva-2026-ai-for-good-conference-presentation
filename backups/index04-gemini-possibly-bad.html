[Image Placeholder: 6 Schools, 6 Countries, $200 Hardware Cost]
The Mission and Numbers.

I am here today with a very specific mission. I am looking for exactly six schools — from six different countries, to pilot the Maker100 Leaders Robotics course for the upcoming academic year.

Three to thirty students per school. The hardware cost is roughly $200 USD per student for the XIAO ML kit and all supporting components. I make absolutely nothing from the hardware, but I only support the platform I have stress-tested for years in my own classroom.

2

Nearly a Decade of Classroom Testing

This is not a prototype. It is a proven course looking for a global stage.

[Image Placeholder: Timeline from Portenta H7 to XIAO ESP32-S3]
Proven Hardware Generation Adaptability.

This course has been running in a real secondary school classroom in British Columbia, Canada for nearly a decade. It has transitioned across two major microcontroller generations, from the Arduino Portenta H7 to the Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32-S3.

The underlying tools are documented in two peer-reviewed arXiv preprints: 2604.23012 and 2604.22834. The curriculum lives openly on GitHub under an MIT license.

Most Students Only Ever Use AI. These Students Build It.

The gap between using AI and understanding it is exactly where this course lives.

[Image Placeholder: Split view of consuming AI vs constructing microcontrollers]
Owning the Full Machine Learning Pipeline.

Artificial intelligence is reshaping every industry, but most students only interact with it as consumers. This pilot offers something genuinely different.

Students collect their own sensor data, train neural networks directly in a browser, and deploy working AI models onto physical microcontrollers — using both Edge Impulse and fully sovereign browser-based software stacks.

## 🎤 Maker100 Leaders Robotics: Presentation Script

**(Target: 16 Slides)**

### **Section 1: The Shock & The Question (The Hook)**

**Slide 1: Title Slide**
"Good morning/afternoon. I’m Jeremy Ellis. And today, I’m not here to talk about another robotics club. I’m here to talk about the future of leadership. I’m here to talk about **Maker100 Leaders Robotics.**"

**Slide 2: The Provocative Question**
"Let me start with a question that I believe every leader, every educator, and every student needs to answer: **Should leaders of the future have a grassroots understanding of Artificial Intelligence?**"

**Slide 3: The Reality Check**
"AI isn't science fiction anymore. It’s reshaping policy, jobs, and our daily lives. But here is the critical gap: **Most people are not just *using* AI; they are not *building* it.** Ignorance in this space is a vulnerability. And our current education system is failing to equip our students with this foundational literacy."

**Slide 4: The Thesis Statement**
"My mission is simple: We are moving beyond consumption. We are moving toward **creation.** We are moving from being AI consumers to becoming AI architects. And that is what Maker100 Leaders Robotics is about."

### **Section 2: The Credibility & The Vision**

**Slide 5: Who Am I? (Briefly)**
"Why should you listen to me? I’m not an academic, and I’m not just an engineer. I’m a Canadian high school teacher who looked at neural networks in the 90s and realized I needed to understand them to teach them. For the last decade, I’ve been an expert in two colliding fields: **WebAI and TinyML.**"

**Slide 6: The Core Philosophy**
"This brings me to the heart of our approach: **LLMs are not cheating tools; they are your teacher assistants.** They are the most powerful, accessible teaching assistants ever created. They allow students to ask the right questions, evaluate the answers, and learn how to prompt the machine—which is the most important AI skill of the next decade."

**Slide 7: The Big Picture Vision**
"Our vision is to create leaders who can bridge the gap between abstract AI theory and tangible, real-world application. Leaders don't just consume technology; they understand its potential, its limitations, and how to deploy it responsibly."

### **Section 3: The Innovation Showcase (The Demos)**

**Slide 8: Innovation 1: AI Anywhere (WebLLM PWA)**
"The biggest barrier to learning AI is access. We solve that with the **WebLLM PWA.** This is a Progressive Web App that brings a powerful LLM directly into your browser. **Watch this.**"
*(Transition to Demo 1)*

**Slide 9: DEMO 1: WebLLM**
*(During Demo 1: Show the PWA running offline. Emphasize: "No internet. No cloud. The intelligence stays on the machine. This is true digital autonomy.")*

**Slide 10: Innovation 2: On-Device Intelligence (TinyML)**
"But what if the internet is down? What if privacy is a concern? We solve that with **TinyML.** We put machine learning directly onto the hardware."
*(Transition to Demo 2)*

**Slide 11: DEMO 2: WebMCU-AI**
*(During Demo 2: Show the XIAO board running a vision model. Emphasize: "This is Edge AI. This is privacy. This is power where it matters most.")*

**Slide 12: The Full Pipeline**
"This isn't just two separate tools. This is the complete pipeline: **From raw sensor data in the browser, to training the model, to compiling it, and deploying it onto a physical device via WebSerial.** Students own the entire stack. No black boxes. No vendor lock-in."

### **Section 4: The Classroom Reality**

**Slide 13: The Hardware**
"The tools are accessible. The core of our course is the **XIAO ESP32-S3 ML Kit.** It’s fingertip-sized, incredibly cheap, and packed with sensors—camera, microphone, motion, everything you need to start building."

**Slide 14: The Learning Structure**
"How do we ensure everyone succeeds? We use a structure built on collaboration, not competition. We use **'Lead Three'**—the first student to solve a hard problem teaches three others. This builds collective knowledge, not hoarding."

**Slide 15: The Student Spectrum**
"We cater to three essential student types: **Users** who gain big-picture leadership; **Makers** who execute and build; and **Engineers** who love the friction and first principles. Every student finds their path to leadership here."

### **Section 5: The Call to Action (The Ask)**

**Slide 16: The Pilot Structure**
"This isn't a theory. This is a proven, adaptable course. We are launching a global pilot with **six schools from six different countries.** We are testing: Does it work with generalists? Does it work with poor internet? Does it scale?"

**Slide 17: The Ask (The Investment)**
"To make this pilot a reality, we need your partnership. The hardware cost is $200 per student. For the right partner, that is a rounding error. We need six schools to generate the undeniable data that unlocks global funding."

**Slide 18: Three Ways to Partner**
"You have three paths to join this revolution: **One:** School budget allocation. **Two:** Sponsor a cohort—cover the hardware for a class immediately. **Three:** Student-led fundraising—teaching them how to advocate for their own education."

**Slide 19: Final Vision**
"We are not just building robots. We are building a generation of leaders who can build, understand, and lead the AI revolution. Let’s stop waiting for the future. Let’s start building it, together. Thank you."

**Slide 20: Contact Slide**
"I am Jeremy Ellis. Let’s meet next year with data. Thank you."
